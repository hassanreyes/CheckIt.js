Get Started with CheckIt

 

Welcome

 

Hello! And welcome to the CheckIt tool.

Checklists are so useful, but create one is not. Sometimes is hard to know how to start; well, don´t worry we have the solution. CheckIt is a communal compilation of checklists where users can find and create checklists for their need and at the same time help others creating a knowledge base.

For the moment users might only create Software Development related checklists.

 

The following image shows the application layout.

1.       Navigation Bar. A convenient way to go around the application, but also let you do a quick search in the checklist compendium.

2.       Working On area. Registered users can have a working on checklist at a time, this area shows an outline of the current authoring checklist.

3.       Navigation Page. The content of this area depends on what are you doing: browsing over checklists, editing your working on checklist, etc.

Everyone can browse the compendium of checklists, but only registered users can author.

 

 

Navigate

 

Learn

Before anything take a look to the Learn page, the link is located at the navigation bar at the top of the screen.

Here you will find useful information about what is checklist and how to create a good one.

Browse

As a non-registered user you are able to browse and search in the checklist compendium. Locate the Browse access at the navigation bar. The following image shows how the catalog of checklists is organized.

At the left of this page you will see a tree of available categories, you can collapse a category node if you required. By selecting a category the list of checklists is updated in order to show all checklists of that category and categories below.

The list of checklists is grouped by category and paged; showing 10 records by page.

Search

The top input field let you enter a string criteria of one or more words; clicking the search button will perform a Full-Text search through all published checklists, returning the result ordered by importance. Create Account

 

So, you can Learn, Browse and Search in the checklist compendium, thats wonderfull and very useful; but what if you what to create your own checklists, share with others, keep your favorites and collaborate with others? Well, you need an account. Its easy, click the sing Up for free button at the Working On area or at the Navigation bar, a screen like the following will let you enter your account information.

 

 

Consider the following rules before create an account.

·         All fields are required.

·         Email must be a valid email address.

·         The user name must be unique. An error message will be shown if not.

·         The password must have at least 6 characters, dont use special o punctuation characters.

 

Dashboard

 

After a successful sing-up in the system you user Dashboard will come up.

 

As you can see the Working On section its being able. This is an outline of your editing checklist, but you will see more of this in the next section.

What can you find in your dashboard? Let see.

·         My Favorites Checklists. A top 5 of your favorite checklists, even when you are not the author or collaborator of them.

·         My Checklists. A top 10 of your checklists, those where you are the author.

Checklist Authoring

 

Checklists Status

 Ok, first you need to know that a checklist may has two status.

·         Draft. This mean that the checklist may not be finished or is waiting for approval. Only the checklist author can publish a checklist. A published checklist will become to draft after any edition. Collaborator can see a draft checklist on their dashboards (Collaborating On).

·         Published. After been a draft, a checklist can be published by and only by the author. Once published it will be able to be browse or find for the others.

Working On

The Working On concept is a restriction where the user can have only one checklist in edition at the time. This restriction let you focus on your work (a very important skill when authoring a checklist).

To create a new empty checklist you can select the New Checklist under the Checklists menu or click the new button in the Working On area. If you already has a checklist in your Working On area you will be ask to dismiss you current checklist. After this the Working On area will be clear and the checklist edition page will come over.

 

 

 

Now you can start typing; after set the title you can just hit the Enter key to advance to checklist description, hit tap again and move to a new Section name, and so on to create your checklist.

 The editor was made for easy use, even when you can use the mouse, the keyboard is the only you need to author you checklist content.

 

 

As you can see in the above image, the Working On area shows you at the time you are typing, the outline of your checklist. Even when you cant type in the Working On area you can remove Items or even entire Sections by clicking on the X next to each one. In the Working On area you can also reorder your entire checklist.

Must of the times you may want to include topics or sections of other related checklists. Dont worry, you dont need to copy-paste; browse or find a desired checklist, now click the little menu button at the front of a Section or Item, the following image shows how to add in a fast and easy way content to your Working On checklist.

 

 

Once you have done editing you checklist, you can save it by clicking any of the two save buttons (little floppy disk icon) but be aware to select a category in the category combo box. After this your new created checklist draft will be shown.

 

 

Now you can keep editing your checklist or review to publish. Remember, in this moment only you can see this checklist, to set able for everybody just click the Publish button next to the This is a draft sing.

You always can edit your own checklist using the edit button at the view checklist page or the list button on the Navigation bar.

 

Checklist Upload

Maybe you already have some checklists, well; you can import them to your CheckIt account. Select the Upload option below the Checklists menu, a page like the following will be show.

Drag and Drop your plain text files to the drop panel or select a file with the Upload Single file button. Be aware to follow the explain rules. A simple file is available in the simple file link. After select the upload file and been validated the system will show the uploaded checklist in the edition page and the Working On are. If you already have a checklist in Working On you will be ask to dismiss the current work.

Collaboration Work

 

One of the main objectives of CheckIt is the collaborative work. Sometimes a checklist may need to be defined by a group of collaborators. As an author of a checklist you can invite others to help you define a checklist. Once you had create a checklist, click the Collaborators button at the View Checklist page, the following dialog will be show.

 

 

The Share dialog will help you to find people, once you locate a registered user you can add it. By default all collaborators have View access to the checklist, to grant a collaborator for edit, click the Edit button in the list of collaborators. To remove an existent collaborator just click the Remove button next to collaborator name. To confirm the changed press Ok.

The collaborator button also show the number of collaborators that a checklist has.

 

As a collaborator you will be able to see the checklist in the Collaborating On section in your Dashboard even if the checklist is a draft.

 

Just as if the shared checklist were yours, you can load on your own Working On area. All users that been Working On a shared checklist will be working on the same Working On checklist, so if someone add a new item to a section all other on line users will see the change on their Working On checklist immediately.

Only the author is able to publish a shared checklist.
